Kalamunda - WA

Date: 25-26 January 2026

Type: forest 1 night quiet hiking hills

Highlights

Intent / Memory

Although a beautiful site, the ground is too dense to put tent pegs into ground so made do with placing hiking bag for weight to keep in place.

Where

Places to Visit

  • Trails Hub Cafe
  • Small cafe 45 mins from hut

Places to Visit

  • Hewitts Hill Hut
  • Hutand site for free overnight camping

Reading

  • Walden - Henry David Thoreau

Notes

Walked the first section of the Bibbulmun track today from Kalamunda to the Hewett's Hill Hut. Left at around 11.30am.  Luckily i downloaded all-trails app as we set out, as about halfway i checked the map and we had wandered a fair way off course and had to double back about a km. Stopped at a cafe to rest and they told us we were only 45 mins from the hut. It’s very relaxing out in the bush. There's a shack you can stay in for free which Pete set his hammock up in as he only has the fly-net and it had a light drizzle. Tried BACK COUNTRY - Moroccan lamb with quinoa freeze dried dinner, it was okay, 2.5 stars maybe. A little bit spicy. Setup my tent in about 10 mins which is okay for the first time. Its more spacious than i realised, though i wouldn’t really suggest 2 people for it. Ground is too dense for pegs but luckily theres little wind to move it. Was reading Henry David Thoreau's Walden for about 45 mins after dinner; Goes well with the cabin feel. Went to bed around 830, couldn’t sleep though. Woke up at 5am and had BACK COUNTRY COOKED BREAKFAST. It was pretty tasty and one meal split between the 2 of us was plenty, 4 stars. Got on our way home, ended up getting an Uber after an hour as not used to trekking multiple days yet.
